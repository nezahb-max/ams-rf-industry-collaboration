Selected examples

Public-facing demonstrations of RF engineering work

Each example is framed as a public-safe technical snapshot. The downloads are intended to support discussion, not to expose full internal project files.

Horn antenna and waveguide feed | 4 GHz

Printed Fan-Beam Horn with WR229 Feed

A 3D-printed horn concept for 4 GHz work with a WR229 interface, supported by measured radiation-pattern results and prototype photos.

  • WR229 internal opening: 58.17 x 29.08 mm
  • Target aperture: 240 x 90 mm
  • Measured scan range: -90 deg to +90 deg with 1 deg step
Download public demo

Waveguide mode converter | 10 GHz / X-band

Rectangular-to-Circular Mode Converter

A published transition with printed and metal prototype evidence, geometry visuals, and return-loss comparison plots.

  • Printed and metallic prototype references
  • S11 comparison between simulation and measurement
  • Suitable as a feed-chain integration example
Download public demo

Lens antenna system | 10 GHz

Dielectric Lens with Circular Horn Feed

A horn-plus-lens system that combines printed hardware with measured focusing behavior and system-level S11 comparison.

  • Documented nominal focus: 100 mm
  • Measured focus reported near 95 +/- 5 mm
  • Includes axial-power and return-loss comparisons
Download public demo

Passive RF target | 10 GHz

Trihedral Corner Reflector Validation

A trihedral corner reflector workflow with STL models, analytic and MoM comparison, and measured-data benchmarking.

  • Analytic peak RCS: 13.27 dBsm
  • MoM boresight result: 14.04 dBsm
  • Includes preview STL and RCS comparison plots
Download public demo

Sensitivity study | 10 GHz to 200 GHz projection

Corner Reflector Tilt Sensitivity Study

A simulation-led study of how one-face tilt changes reflector response, including a high-frequency projection.

  • About 10 dB loss near 12 deg tilt at fixed boresight
  • 100 GHz projection reaches -10 dB near 2.19 deg
  • 3 dB tracked-peak angle remains near 8.60 deg through 200 GHz
Download public demo
