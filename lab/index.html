About the lab

Applied Magnetic Systems Laboratory

The Applied Magnetic Systems Laboratory (AMS Lab) is a research group within the Department of Electrical Engineering and Electronics at Ariel University. The laboratory conducts experimental and theoretical research in applied electromagnetics and is equipped with RF and microwave measurement instruments, a W-band radar platform, magnetic field mapping tools, 3D printing facilities, and electromagnetic simulation software.

Research areas

Main directions of work

Projects combine analytical modelling, numerical simulation, precision fabrication, and experimental validation.

1

Periodic Magnetic Structures

Design, fabrication, and characterization of helical and planar undulators for free-electron lasers and compact radiation sources in the EUV and X-ray regimes.

2

Millimeter-Wave Radar

Millimeter-wave radar systems for target classification, micro-Doppler analysis, and remote sensing, including atmospheric propagation studies.

3

Antennas and RF Components

Design and characterization of compact antennas, waveguides, metamaterial lenses, and passive RF components using simulation and additive manufacturing.
